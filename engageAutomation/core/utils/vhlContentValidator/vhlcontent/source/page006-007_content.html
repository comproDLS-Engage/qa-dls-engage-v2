BEFORE YOU READ

Reading Strategy IDENTIFY SUPPORTING EVIDENCE FOR A CLAIM

You will read a personal narrative.

As you read, you will find information in the text and images. The images include:

  • photographs
  • graphs
  • primary sources (images or documents from the time period the author is writing about)

The author uses the images to support her claim.

You can collect information about text and visual support in an organizer.

Academic Vocabulary

claim (noun)

DEFINITION
the main argument or point of view

EXAMPLE
The author's claim about voting rights was supported with facts, examples, and graphs.

WORD FORMS
claims, claimed, claiming

Don't Pay for DNA Testing; Have Some FUN Instead!

by Pierre Bernard

1 Did you know that genes carry your identity in DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid)? But my identity is not only in my genes. It's made up of my experiences with family, friends, and strangers. It is shaped by what I've seen, heard, and felt. My identity is not just my DNA.

2 DNA testing is a billion-dollar industry. The number of people who have taken DNA tests quadrupled between 2015 and 2021.

3 DNA test companies promise that people can use DNA tests to discover their heritage. But I don't think the tests are worth it. Perhaps it's interesting to learn something about your heritage, like I did, but it is a waste of money. I could have invested in a fun experience instead.

4 In addition, scientists are not confident in the results, which are too simple. Scientists argue that test results do not show definite answers. So, think twice before you send your DNA to a test site. It may tell you that your ancestors may or may not have lived in Ivory Coast at one time. But it won't reveal everything that makes you so awesome!
